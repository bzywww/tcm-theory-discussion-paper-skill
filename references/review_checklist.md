# Review Checklist

## Topic and Argument

- Does the paper begin from a real clinical problem?
- Is the disease/stage/symptom-state precise?
- Is the core TCM mechanism visible in the title or early argument?
- Does the paper avoid repeating recent target-journal axes?
- Is there a clear academic increment?

## Theory Structure

- Does the article define its object before pathogenesis claims?
- Do classical sources support specific argument links?
- Is the pathogenesis a chain rather than a list?
- Is the core mechanism phrase concise and treatment-guiding?
- Are variants or syndrome differentiations tied to the core mechanism?

## Modern Mechanism

- Does modern evidence explain a specific TCM link?
- Is modern material limited to necessary concepts?
- Does every modern mechanism return to a TCM concept?
- Does the paper avoid molecular/pathway dumping?

## Treatment and Formula

- Are treatment principles derived from pathogenesis?
- Are formulas organized by syndrome and method?
- Does each formula explanation return to the core mechanism?
- Are regulation, rehabilitation, or lifestyle suggestions tied to TCM logic?

## Language and Style

- Are section headings academic claims?
- Are paragraphs single-function and readable?
- Are chatty, explanatory, or internal phrases removed?
- Are low-value phrases such as “由此可见”, “该理论”, “本文认为”, and “例如” removed or rewritten?
- Are drafting markers such as “【待补】” or “【中医】” removed?

## Deliverable Readiness

- Title, abstract, keywords, headings, body, and references are complete.
- All citations in body match reference entries.
- No unsupported clinical claims or invented cases appear.
- The article does not masquerade as a clinical study if no cases/data are provided.
