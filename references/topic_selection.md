# Topic Selection

## Selection Logic

Choose topics by the sequence:

1. **Clinical true problem**: a common, difficult, or under-explained clinical phenomenon.
2. **Precise cut**: disease stage, symptom cluster, treatment dilemma, recurrence pattern, recovery problem, or pathogenesis link.
3. **Orthodox TCM root**: zangxiang, qi-blood-fluid-spirit, six channels, ying-wei, triple burner, meridians, pathogen transformation, or treatment principle.
4. **Modern disease carrier**: a named disease, population, state, or chronic disease context.
5. **Academic increment**: what this explains beyond existing articles.

Avoid starting with “从某理论论某病” unless the clinical problem is already precise.

## Strong Topic Pattern

Use one of these shapes:

- “从[TCM mechanism]探析[modern disease/stage/symptom-state]的病机与辨治”
- “基于[orthodox TCM theory]探讨[clinical state]的形成与治法”
- “[core mechanism phrase]: [clinical object]的病机与辨治探析”
- “从[classic phrase/concept]论[clinical problem]的[formation/transmission/treatment]”

## Topic Acceptance Tests

A topic is ready only if all are true:

- It names a specific clinical carrier and a precise problem.
- It has 3-5 classical sources that each support a different argument link.
- It can produce a pathogenesis chain, not only a list of factors.
- It can lead naturally to treatment principles and formulas.
- It avoids the main axes already heavily published in the target journal.

## Repetition Risk Screen

Compare the planned topic against recent target-journal titles and major published axes. Mark high risk when the topic mainly repeats:

- the same disease + same theory phrase;
- the same core mechanism phrase;
- the same treatment principle sequence;
- a famous professor-experience article without new disease-stage or mechanism cut.

When risk is high, narrow by disease stage, symptom-state, mechanism link, or treatment dilemma.
