# Article Structure

## Preferred Theory-Discussion Flow

Use this structure for most Chinese TCM theory discussion papers:

1. **Clinical problem introduction**: name the unresolved clinical phenomenon and why it deserves theory discussion.
2. **Name and domain**: define the article's object, disease stage, symptom-state, and boundaries.
3. **Classical basis**: introduce only the classic concepts needed for the later pathogenesis chain.
4. **Mechanism/pathogenesis derivation**: show how the problem arises and persists.
5. **Core pathogenesis condensation**: compress the derivation into a short, memorable TCM mechanism phrase.
6. **Syndrome differentiation or transformation**: explain variants, stages, or subtypes.
7. **Treatment principle derivation**: derive the methods directly from the pathogenesis.
8. **Formula and medicine logic**: map formulas and herbs to the treatment principles.
9. **Regulation/rehabilitation/clinical implication**: include only if it follows from the pathogenesis.
10. **Conclusion**: restate the new explanation and clinical value without overclaiming.

## Section Title Rules

Section titles must be academic claims, not task labels.

Weak titles:

- 理论基础
- 病机分析
- 现代机制
- 治疗思路
- 方药配伍

Stronger titles:

- 心脉受损则神失所舍
- 病中所惊可留滞于神
- 湿浊困脾则清阳不升
- 营卫失和则神不内守
- 和脉安神以复其舍
- 化痰泄浊以畅其机

## Paragraph Function Discipline

Each paragraph should mainly do one job:

- define the object;
- interpret one classical source;
- connect one clinical phenomenon to one mechanism;
- advance one pathogenesis link;
- derive one treatment principle;
- explain one formula's logic.

Avoid paragraphs that mix source review, modern mechanism, TCM pathogenesis, treatment, and clinical advice at once.

## Core Pathogenesis Phrase

A good phrase has 2-4 parts, clear order, and treatment value.

Examples of shapes:

- “[root damage], [spirit dysfunction], [qi transformation disorder]”
- “[deficiency as root], [pathogen as branch], [stasis/toxin/phlegm as transformation]”
- “[organ function failure], [qi-blood-fluid transformation], [spirit/meridian consequence]”

Do not coin phrases that sound elegant but cannot guide treatment.
